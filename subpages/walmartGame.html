Walmart Game Text-Based Game

Access to code

A brief description of the game: Text-based adventure game where the player is a child who is sent to search through aisles in a Walmart for Sriracha and bring it back to the cashier before they finish billing!

For the features we implemented...

  • Resource logic: limited amount of time - to stress the user out and make them more confused, we had a time limit of 200 seconds. If they did not make it back to the cashier in 200 seconds with the sriracha, they lost the game!
  • Penalty system: We also had a penalty system, where if they responded incorrectly to the question “Is the sriracha here?”, they would be redirected back to the cashier and need to 'restart' the game
  • Music, typewriter, sleep & duration: We also had beeping music playing in the introduction to produce the ambience of a grocery store. The typewriter also produced a cool UI effect with the text, and we used the sleep & duration functions in rust.
  • Title screen: We implemented a 'title screen' when the player starts the game. It displays the title of our game, as well as a cool graphic made with ascii symbols. In this title screen, we also place our user in the setting of the game through description.
  • Some kind of interactive interface element: The way that users interact with our game to navigate through the aisles is through user input, where we show the users what options they have (yes/no input && directions input) and the game responds to their input.

Please feel free to check out text-based game out on your coding environment!
